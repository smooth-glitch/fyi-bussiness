    How can I buy a bat without holding it first?

    This is the #1 worry for every cricketer. We bridge this gap in two ways:

    • Video Analysis: We don't just send photos. We send you a video of the ping test and the balance check on a scale so you can see the pickup.
    • The "Pickup" Guarantee: A 1180g bat can feel like 1250g if the balance is wrong. We specifically source willow with a higher spine and lighter toe to ensure the "pickup weight" is always lighter than the scale weight.

    Is this genuine English Willow? There are many fakes online.

    Absolutely. We source directly from manufacturers in Meerut who supply to international brands. We don't just "dropship"—Hari personally visits the factory to hand-select clefts.

    The Proof: Genuine English Willow is lighter and has a distinct grain structure compared to Kashmir Willow or "fake" willow. We encourage you to show our bat to any professional coach or seasoned player; if they can prove it's not genuine English Willow, we will refund 2x your money.

    Will a Grade 1 bat last longer than a Grade 3?

    Actually, it's often the opposite.

    Grade 1 willow is soft, pure, and performs instantly (high ping), but because it is softer, it can be more fragile. Grade 3 is harder and might take longer to open up, but it is often more durable.

    We recommend Grade 1/Player Grade for match use only, and Grade 2/3 for players who want a "workhorse" bat for nets and matches combined.

    What if my bat breaks? (The Handle vs. Toe Reality)

    Cricket bats are wood, not steel. If a 140kph ball hits the toe or edge at a bad angle, any bat can crack.

    • Handle Breaks: Covered. We will replace the handle for free within 3 months.
    • Surface Cracks/Edge Damage: This is normal wear and tear. We advise on how to fix minor damage with glue/tape.
    • Storm/Moisture Damage: Not covered. (Don't leave your bat in the car trunk!)

    Can I copy a specific player's profile (e.g., Kohli/Dhoni)?

    Yes. We can replicate profiles. However, remember that international players use extremely dense wood that might be too heavy for the average club player.

    If you want a "Dhoni profile" (heavy bottom), we will adjust the spine height so you get that shape but at a weight (e.g., 1190g) that you can actually swing fast.
